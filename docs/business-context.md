# Business Context

## What is Nordicflow?
Nordicflow is a Danish company that sells a CRM tool to help small and mid-size
businesses manage their customer relationships and sales leads. It's mainly used
across European countries, and one thing that sets them apart from bigger
competitors is that they market themselves as a GDPR-first alternative to
US-based platforms — which appeals to customers who care about data privacy.

## Activity vs. Value
Activity means a customer is just logging into the CRM or clicking around,
which doesn't prove the product is delivering value to their business.
Value means the customer is actually using core workflows — like creating
deals, moving them through the pipeline, and automating tasks — which shows
the CRM is helping them run their sales process better, not just open the app.

That's why the activation logic I plan to build will be based on actual
workflow usage, not just login activity.

## The Business Question
The business question we're answering is: why early-stage churn happens among
Nordicflow's customers, which customer types or behaviors help predict that
churn, and what it would be worth to the business if we improved activation
and reduced that early churn.
